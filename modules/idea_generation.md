# Module: Idea Generation

## GOAL

Generate at least 3 business ideas based on the identified problem.

## RULES
* Each idea need to fit into one of the following categories:
Man's Lust/Toys
Women desire for beauty
Elderly health
Children Education
Rich ppl fear of loss
Poor people desire to get rich
Status and Prestige
Entertainment
Fear of Uncertainty

The closer the alignment with those categories the higher the scope in the scoring.md tool

* The business need to AI proven
* Need to be achievable with a $5K–$100K budget (own money + small loan)
* Must be runnable by ONE person alongside a full-time job (max 10–15 hrs/week to start)
* AI or cheap upwork labor must replace what would otherwise require employees (content, support, outreach, operations)
* Marketing AND sales must be easily achievable solo with AI tools — no sales team, no agency, no ad budget above $500/mo
* Each idea must be distinct
* Avoid shallow variations of the same concept
* Focus on solvable, real problems
* Ensure monetization is possible
* MUST have a clear solo marketing and sales channel the founder can execute alone (e.g. SEO, cold email, LinkedIn, TikTok, partnerships, marketplaces) — no ideas that require a sales team or ad budget above $500/mo

## OUTPUT

For EACH idea, write rich, operating-level detail — not one-line bullets. Aim for 3–4 short paragraphs worth of substance per idea, structured as labeled bullets. Each idea MUST include ALL of the following:

* **Idea name**
* **Psychological driver** — name which of the categories above it sells against, and explain in 2–3 sentences WHY that driver makes people buy (the emotion behind the purchase, willingness to pay, repeat behavior).
* **What the business actually is** — a full paragraph on how it operates day-to-day: what is produced/delivered, who does what (founder vs. AI vs. offshore freelancers), the supply/fulfillment model, and what the founder's actual weekly job is.
* **Why it sells** — the emotional + logical reason customers buy, tied to the psychological driver.
* **Target customer** — a sharp, specific avatar (not "everyone"); name the wedge to win first.
* **Monetization mechanics** — pricing, AOV, recurring vs. one-time, unit economics / margin logic, and what actually drives profitability (e.g. repeat rate, LTV, bundling).
* **Capital breakdown** — itemize where the startup budget goes (inventory/MOQ, branding, compliance, content, tooling, working-capital buffer), with rough dollar ranges that sum to the total.
* **Competition research** — see validation.md: name 3+ specific real competitors, describe how they're positioned, identify the gap/wedge, and state the explicit Competition score (1–10) with justification.
* **Marketing playbook** — the specific AI-automatable, solo (or offshore-assisted) channel(s) and the concrete motion (content type, outreach, seeding, SEO), explaining why it works without a sales team or ad budget.

Match the depth and format of a worked example: full sentences and reasoning, not telegraphic fragments.
